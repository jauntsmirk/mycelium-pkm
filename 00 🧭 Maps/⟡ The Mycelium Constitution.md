## I. Ontological Premise

Knowledge does not grow linearly.  
It propagates, branches, decomposes, recombines, and resurfaces.

This system models knowledge as **fungal ecology**:
- Invisible most of the time
- Networked beneath the surface
- Expressed through occasional, visible fruiting

The vault known as **Mycelium** exists to support this ecology.

---

## II. Core Law

> **Folders are metaphorical habitats.**  
> **Notes are explicit scientific specimens.**

Metaphor governs _where_ things live.  
Clarity governs _what_ things are.

## IV. Rules of Practice

1. Write to understand, not to record.
2. Prefer linking over duplication.
3. Let structure grow slower than knowledge.
4. Refactor deliberately and infrequently.
5. Protect clarity over aesthetics (function over form).

---

## V. Amendment Protocol

This Constitution is designed to be stable, not static.
Amendments are permitted under the following conditions:
1. An amendment is made deliberately, not reactively.
2. An amendment reflects sustained usage over time, not a single moment of friction.
3. An amendment clarifies existing structure rather than multiplying it.

All amendments must be:
- Dated
- Briefly justified
- Additive or corrective, never cosmetic

Amendments are recorded at the end of this document under a clearly labeled section.

## VI. Closing Principle

This system is not a monument.  
It is a living organism.

If it grows, it is correct.  
If it adapts, it is healthy.  
If it supports future fruiting, it is successful.
